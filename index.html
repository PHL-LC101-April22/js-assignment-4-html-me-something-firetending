Get Up & Go:
Runnin Up That Hill

No matter the size, the cheapest and closest spot is the best place to get started! (not pictured: the hill of my neighbor's front lawn) Heading straight from school to the hill at dusk on cold winter days laid the groundwork for my obsession. Glimmers of progress and my dad's encouragement got me through it by the soaked seat of my pants, so now I can push him until our legs are beat and nothing sounds better than getting these boots off.

Getting Better:
Driving Further

The better you get, the faster you hit top to bottom, the more mountain you want. Day trips to New Hampshire and Vermont taught me to love new terrain with open woods and deeper snow. If you're lucky you're not in the driver's seat (thanks Dad), heading out at 5AM when the temp reads 0° and it's only getting colder heading north. The tickets weren't cheap but the ice was always free, god bless 'em.

Going West:
Diving Deeper

The Western US is another league entirely. Huge mountains catch heavy clouds to dump the light, airy snow they're known for in heaps. That takes a deep powder day from slogging in the East to surfing in the West. My first trip out I got sidelined by a broken wrist and my Dad had to get enough turns in for the both of us. Find me and my best ski buddy floating through the endless glades, bowls, and basins of these behemoths and we'll have a time you won't soon forget.
